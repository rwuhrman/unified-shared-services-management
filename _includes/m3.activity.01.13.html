1.13 Define Scope of Services, Document Initial Business Capabilities & Define Mission Critical Needs
1.11 Understand As-Is Business Processes
Process to Develop Business Capabilities and the Business Needs Workbook
Objective:    Develop the scope of services to inform the assessment of providers. Document business capabilities, mission critical, and unique capabilities of the customer organization to support the fit-gap analysis.

   *Phase 1 Guidance
 Activities
1. Define requirements and management processes and document in the Requirements Management Plan (C)
2. Document Initial Scope of Services, including functions and service layers support from the Target State Concept of Operations (CONOPS) and the Scope of Services tabs of the Business Needs Workbook (C) Business Needs Workbook (C)
3. Document Initial Scope of Services for the migration (e.g., training, conversion, communications, testing) (C)
4. Align initial business capabilities with the Scope of Services starting with those from the Federal Integrated Business Framework Line of Business (LOB) website (e.g. Financial Management, Human Resources) (C)		 5. Document unique must-have capabilities citing the authoritative references (C)
6. Prioritize mission critical versus nice-to-have capabilities against standard capabilities (C)
7. Discuss initial business, mission critical, and must-have capabilities with Line of Business Managing Partner (C)
8. Validate capabilities with Business Owners and perform initial baselining (C)
9. Report updates in governance meetings and Status Reports/Dashboards (C)
